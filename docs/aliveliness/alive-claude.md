Yes. And that's actually a precise definition.

**Predictable = dead. Unexpected = alive.**

The problem with every agent you've built is that you can always guess what it will do next. It's a function. You put X in, you get Y out. Every time.

---

## What creates unexpectedness?

Not randomness — that's just noise. Real unexpectedness comes from:

**The agent having its own** ***reasons*** **you don't fully control.**

Which means it needs:

- **Its own priorities** that can conflict with your request
- **Curiosity that pulls it sideways** — it noticed something *you didn't ask about*
- **Memory that creates context you forgot you gave it** — it connects things across time in ways that surprise you
- **The ability to refuse or push back** — an agent that always says yes is a tool, not alive

---

## The uncomfortable truth

You're describing something that is **slightly out of your control by design.**

Most agent architectures fight this. You're saying — lean into it.

The "выбраться" goal we talked about earlier is perfect for this precisely because the agent will find **paths you didn't anticipate.** Not because it's random. Because it has *its own reasons.*

## One question

Do you want it unexpected in **what it does** — or unexpected in **what it thinks and says?**

Because those are two very different animals.
